Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani


Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani, often shortened as Maulana Bhashani, was a Bengali Politician. Maulana Bhashani was popularly known by the honorary title Mozlum Jananeta meaning leader of the oppressed for his lifelong stance advocating for the poor. He is considered as one of the main pillars of Bangladeshi independence of 1971.

About the Legend

☛ Mohammad Abdul Hamid Khan was born at Dhangara, a village in the Sirajganj mahakuma (lit. 'a subdistrict') of the Pabna district in Bengal, British India (present-day Sirajganj District in Bangladesh), to Hajji Mohammad Sharafat Khan and Mazirannesa Bibi in a Bengali Muslim family.
☛ Following independence, Bhashani wanted to play the role of a responsible opposition. [citation needed] The progressive forces quickly gathered around him and strengthened the National Awami Party with Kazi Zafar Ahmed as its General Secretary. But soon factional differences among the progressive forces emerged and weakened Bhashani's position.
☛ Bhashani was highly critical of the authoritarian style of the Awami League and BAKSAL government.[35] He also warned Sheikh Mujibur Rahman against the ever-rising corruption and political chaos in his own government happening unbeknownst to him. Bhashani was deeply shocked at the killing of Mujib, for whom he had a lot of fatherly affection and his family members. The person who conveyed the news of Mujib's demise described how Bhashani cried and then went to his prayer room to offer prayer.
☛ In the early 1950s, he felt that an integrated Pakistan was no longer maintainable due to the hegemony of West Pakistan. At the Kagmari Conference, he bade farewell to West Pakistan by saying As-salamu alaykum which soon became a reference quote.[28] He declined to participate in the national election of 1970 saying that it would only help perpetuate rule by West Pakistan. From 1969 his favourite slogans were Swadhin Bangla Zindabad and Azad Bangla Zindabad. His dream of an independent Bengal came true when Bangladesh was established as an independent nation-state in 1971. He advocated for the separation of State and religion. He was a pious Muslim who was in favour of socialism. He spoke out against Bangladesh Jamaat-e-Islami and its politics.

☛ Bhashani is regarded as the proponent of anti-imperialist, non-communal and left-leaning politics by his admirers in present-day Bangladesh and beyond.[45][46] In 2013, the Awami League Government of Bangladesh reduced his presence in school curricula.[47] In 2004, Bhashani was ranked number 8 in BBC's poll of the Greatest Bengali of all time
